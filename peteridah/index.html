Insight
Agentic AI is moving faster than governance. Most organisations deploying AI agents have no clear map of what actions are authorised, who is accountable, or how to demonstrate that to a regulator. That gap — between capability and control — is where I work.
The problem is not building agentic systems. It is building them with provable authority boundaries. Without identity-bound delegation, runtime kill switches, and evidence-pack outputs at decision points, you have a capable but ungovernable system. That is where architectures break down in regulated environments.
